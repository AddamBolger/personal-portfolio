Thoughts and feelings on Manchester's Digital Industry

M anchester’s Digital Industry is growing more and more by the second. There are alot of jobs in a wide range of disciplines. I have looked at a few reports and sources following this discovery and the following will look at how this impacts me as a Front-end dev.

Skills

One of the report’s focus points in relation to my field I have been astounded at the rate of expected growth for Developers as it is the expected to grow 20% according to Manchester Digital’s Skills Audit (MDSA). This is supported by Technation’s 2018 report which states “Between 2014 and 2017 employment in the digital tech sector increased by 13.2%.” This means there is an abundant of jobs in the sector even allowing versatility in job applications, possibly creating a competitive employment environment.

Of that growth in the sector the demand of JavaScript is very high, according to MDSA 32% of the employers who had taken part in the audit are looking for developers in JavaScript. Similarly Cathcart Associates found this similar demand in the language.

However none of the reports mention React in anyway yet the Manchester Digital audit mentions another JavaScript library; Angular which leaves an ambiguous point on React as to whether it is in demand or not, which from look at the alerts I have set myself up to receive regarding newly posted Dev jobs, is on the rise yet but the report mentions Angular.

Opportunities

Another point I would like to bring up from the MDSA is how many businesses are actively recruiting graduates. According to the report the figure sits at 70%, meaning there is a gap in the industry for graduates to enter, meaning that graduates with plans to enter the digital sector are more likely to secure a role.

Furthering on from this statistic is 48% of businesses are even running their own graduate scheme within the company. This allows for employers to take a freshly graduated Developer for example, and shape them into the developer they want them to be. Not only will this make sure that the jobs gap is filled up but will also provide a feeling of loyalty between the graduate and the company, instilling a certain measure of trust between the two. If the graduate feels they are being helped and guided by the business they are more likely to stay and thus creates a valuable member of the team for the business.

However a statistic mentioned in the MDSA was only 13% of all businesses asked to take part in the audit felt that the graduates had the right soft skills and technical knowledge to be ready for the workplace. Despite the graduate schemes in place the figure shows that student’s upon leaving are not in the best position to start working at the expected level the business expects. If more businesses were to talk to students and advise them on what they were looking for from their graduates the unit modules could be changed to fit this need.

Closing thoughts:

With many employers looking at graduates in a highly competitive sector where the slightest advantage can make all the difference it can be a very daunting sector to go into. Even now talking to Junior Developers or past graduates can make me seem to stand head and shoulders below their capabilities. This gives way to a feeling of impostor syndrome, this is the feeling that a person has when they feel in their mind: “I don’t belong in this place.” Or “I’m not good enough to be here.” These feelings can stand in the way of graduates and even students engaging in the sector or other developers.

This feeling was touched upon by Christopher Murphy showing how it can impact anyone in any role, regardless on level of expertise from novice or recent graduate to Senior roles. Christopher even goes as far as to draw comparisons to a house of cards, which personally I can relate to. Thinking that I’m one question away from the whole stack of cards falling down around me.

Despite this underlying feeling of self-dread. I am feeling very excited of the future, I am happy with choosing to be a Front-end Dev, and I’m very happy to have chosen to come home, to Manchester to do this.
